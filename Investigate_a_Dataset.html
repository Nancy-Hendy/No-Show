Introduction¶

In this project, I will be analyzing a dataset that collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about each patient are included in each row of this dataset. So, I will try to find out the factors that are most related to the     presence/ absence of patients using this analytical project.

Some questions that I need to answer are:¶

  1. Relation of Attendance to Patients' Neighbourhoods?
  2. Relation of Attendance to Different types of Chronic diseases?
  3. Relation of Attendance to Patients' age? Relation of Attendance to Patients' Gender?
  4. Does sending an reminder SMS increase patients' attendance?
  5. Relation of Attendance to Patients' Gender?
  6. Do patients recieving Borsa Familia tend to attend more or less?

def Attendance(Disease):
    pd.crosstab(df[Disease], df.Attended, normalize='columns').plot(kind='bar',alpha = 0.5, colormap='Paired')
    plt.title('Proportion of Attendance in {} patients'.format(Disease))
    plt.xlabel('{}'.format(Disease))
    plt.ylabel('Proportion')
    return plt.show();

Conclusions¶

  1. .Attendance is highly affected by the neighbourhood of the patient. Jardim Camburi being the one with the highest number of overall patients, and also highest proportion of attendance.
  2. Attendance is higher in patients with Hypertension and Diabetes.
  3. Age seems to be realted to the attendance of patients.Age groups 0 to 5 and >45 are the highest proportion of attending patients.
  4. SMS was sent to a very small proportion of patients, so its data cannot be a good indicator for our data analysis.
  5. Attendance is higher in females.
  6. Bolsa Familia Scholarship patients are less likely to attend their appointments.

Limitations:¶

  1. SMS data is not enough as it has been sent to less than half of the patients.
  2. The data taken is taken over a very limited time frame, so can't be enough to make predictions of future non-showing patients.
